Lahore - City of Gardens

The cultural capital of Pakistan, rich in Mughal heritage and living traditions.

Badshahi Mosque

[ Image: Badshahi Mosque ]

Built in 1673 by Mughal Emperor Aurangzeb, the Badshahi Mosque is one of the largest mosques in the world. Its red sandstone facade, towering minarets, and vast courtyard capable of holding over 100,000 worshippers make it an architectural masterpiece. The intricate marble inlay work reflects the pinnacle of Mughal craftsmanship.

Lahore Fort (Shahi Qila)

[ Image: Lahore Fort ]

A UNESCO World Heritage Site, Lahore Fort spans over 20 hectares and contains 21 notable monuments built across different Mughal eras. Highlights include the Sheesh Mahal (Palace of Mirrors), Naulakha Pavilion, and the Alamgiri Gate. The fort stands as a testament to the grandeur of the Mughal Empire at its height.

Shalimar Gardens

[ Image: Shalimar Gardens ]

Commissioned by Emperor Shah Jahan in 1641, the Shalimar Gardens are a UNESCO-listed Mughal garden spread across three terraced levels. The gardens feature hundreds of fountains, marble pavilions, and lush greenery. Once a royal retreat, they remain a symbol of Mughal landscape architecture and continue to attract visitors worldwide.
