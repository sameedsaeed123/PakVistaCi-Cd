Karachi - City of Lights

Pakistan's largest city, a vibrant metropolis where the Arabian Sea meets urban energy.

Clifton Beach

[ Image: Clifton Beach ]

Clifton Beach is Karachi's most iconic seafront destination, stretching along the Arabian Sea coastline. It is a hub of activity — from camel and horse rides to street food stalls serving famous Karachi chaat. Sea View comes alive in the evenings when families gather to enjoy the sea breeze and the sound of waves on the shore.

Mohatta Palace

[ Image: Mohatta Palace ]

Built in 1927 by Shivratri Mohatta as a summer residence, Mohatta Palace blends Rajput and Mughal architectural styles. Its pink Jodhpur stone facade, domed towers, and arched verandas make it one of Karachi's most photographed landmarks. Today it functions as a museum housing Pakistani art, jewellery, and Partition-era artefacts.

Pakistan Maritime Museum

[ Image: Pakistan Maritime Museum ]

Located near PNS Karsaz, the Pakistan Maritime Museum preserves the naval history of Pakistan. It houses the retired submarine PNS Hangor, naval aircraft, artillery, and extensive exhibits on maritime heritage. It is one of the largest open-air maritime museums in South Asia, popular with families, students, and history enthusiasts.
