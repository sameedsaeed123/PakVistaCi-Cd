Hunza Valley

A jewel of the Karakoram, where towering peaks meet serene turquoise lakes.

Rakaposhi Viewpoint

[ Image: Rakaposhi Mountain ]

Rakaposhi, standing at 7,788 metres, is one of the most spectacular peaks in the Karakoram range. The viewpoint near Minapin village offers breathtaking panoramic views of the mountain's imposing north face. On clear days visitors can see glaciers cascading down the slopes, drawing mountaineers and photographers from across the globe.

Attabad Lake

[ Image: Attabad Lake ]

Formed in 2010 after a massive landslide blocked the Hunza River, Attabad Lake is renowned for its striking turquoise waters set against stark rocky terrain. The lake stretches approximately 21 kilometres and has become one of Pakistan's most popular tourist destinations, offering boat rides, kayaking, and surreal submerged tree views.

Baltit Fort

[ Image: Baltit Fort ]

Perched high above Karimabad, Baltit Fort is over 700 years old and served as the residence of the Mirs (rulers) of Hunza. Restored by the Aga Khan Trust for Culture, the fort offers a fascinating glimpse into royal history. From its battlements, visitors are rewarded with sweeping views of Hunza Valley, Rakaposhi, and Ultar Sar peaks.
